[Complete Guide] How to Identify Safe Websites for Kids (What is COPPA?)

2026.01.25 | Lifehack & Tech

"I let my child use my phone, and they clicked on a weird ad..."
"I was worried when an inappropriate commercial played on YouTube."

Have you ever experienced this?
In raising digital natives, managing the risk of "where (which environment)" they view content is just as important as "what" they view.

This article explains "how to identify safe websites" and the key law "COPPA" for adults and parents who want to improve their IT literacy.

What is COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act)?

COPPA is a United States federal law, but it has become the de facto global standard for web services, including Google and Apple.

  • Personal information of children under 13 must not be collected without parental consent.
  • Behavioral targeting ads aimed at children must not be displayed.

In other words, sites and apps that claim to be "COPPA Compliant" are "safe zones" designed to prevent unintended data extraction and inappropriate tracking ads.

3 Must-Read Tips to Identify Safe Sites

So, how can you tell at a glance? Here are three checkpoints for beginners.

1. Check the Privacy Policy

Look at the bottom (footer) of the page. Check the "Privacy Policy" link for statements like "We do not collect data from children" or "Cookie usage is restricted."

2. Check the Type of Ads

While ad-free is ideal, it can be difficult for free services.
The important thing is "what kind of ads are shown." COPPA-compliant sites are configured to show only safe, context-based ads related to the site's content, rather than ads based on user browsing history.
